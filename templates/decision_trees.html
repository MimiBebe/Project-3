Decision Trees

For the decision trees, the strongest influence on scores was tree depth: trees with unlimited nodes (group (a)) scored lowest in all cases. They may be overtrained on the training as opposed to the test split.

The highest scores otherwise were found using Total Income or log(Total Income) with a limited set of other factors (3b, 3d, 5b, 5d; scores highlighted). Removing the minor factors (as determined by the Random Forest analysis) may reduce the risk of overtraining.

There was no difference between runs using Total Income and log(Total Income). Treating Applicant and Co-Applicant Income separately (4b, 4c, 4d) resulted in lower scores.
