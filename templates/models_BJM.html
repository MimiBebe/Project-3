Prediction Model Exploration

*** different types of models were tested: ***, KNeighbors Classifier, Decision Trees, Random Forests, and Neural Networks.
Within each, several different factor combinations were tried:
-- Income was treated as Total Income, as separate terms for Applicant and Co-Applicant Income, and/or as log(Total Income).
-- Because we do not know what Loan Amounts or Loan Terms the applicants requested vs. what they received, making them a possible combination of input and output (an applicant requesting too large a loan might be denied despite otherwise favorable attributes), some models were tested both with and without these terms.
-- The Random Forest runs found several terms (Gender, Education, Self_Employed, Married) of consistently low importance, so some of the other models were tested both with and without this set.
Factors specific to the different model types were also varied, discussed below.
Overall, most test accuracies were in the range of 0.7 - 0.8, and income and credit score were the primary deciding variables. Including more terms or longer model runs did not necessarily improve accuracy; this could be an example of "overtraining", with models too well adjusted to the training subset to work well on the test subset.

KNeighbors Classifier

For the KNN model runs, only the factor combinations discussed above were tested.
Train and test scores generally required inclusion of more neighbors to converge when fewer factors were included, but there were no strong trends in test accuracy scores.
